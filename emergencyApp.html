Hurricane Emergency App

Hurricanes hit the US every year. Whether they cause big destruction or only minor damages they scare citizens and bring uncertainty. LifeBoat is a peer-to-peer app that stimulates communication and help for people in affected locations.

LifeBoat

LifeBoat consists of an application that allows citizens located in areas that have been or may be hit by hurricanes, to locate and/or drive other citizens to shelters and help those that are facing debris emergencies.


Research

Guerrilla Research

The team started with a broad scope problem of designing a peer economy app that would help victims of disasters. To better understand this scope we conducted guerrilla research trying to find out more information related to natural disasters, in general, and to understand how people who had been through them attempted to deal with the situation.


Ideation

Scenarios

After finding out more about the needs and difficulties of people experiencing emergency situations and natural disasters, the team created 20 scenarios representing possible solutions. The scenarios represented solutions for tackling previously identified issues, to implement shelter transportation and donation pick-up schedules, for debris clearing, and for real-time donation monitoring.


Personas

In order to better understand future customers, our team created two personas, each representing one side of the peer economy app, the victim and the provider. These helped us better to visualize potential users and to develop final scenarios.

Jeff the volunteer

Linda the customer



Jeff, the volunteer, is 25 years old and lives in Immokalee, FL. He works at a local construction firm doing manual labor and drives a Ford F150 pickup truck. He has already experienced three hurricanes during his time in Florida and has served as a volunteer after each of them; he primarily transported people and heavy/large donations to shelters and cleared large debris that blocked people from leaving their homes. Jeff has helped people not only in his own city, but also in nearby ones.

Linda is 36 years old and lives in Naples, FL. She is a single mother of a 7 year old boy and a 8 year old girl and works as an administrative assistant at the local elementary school. Her mother is 75 and lives by herself in a nearby apartment. Since her mother has trouble walking, Linda and her kids visit her regularly at the apartment. Linda has been through various hurricanes and has never been evacuated. She has visited a donation center once and usually brings her mom from the apartment to her house during hurricanes.


Final Scenarios

With the personas in mind, the team adapted three final scenarios. Two related to post-hurricane situations and one related to a pre-hurricane situation.

The pre-hurricane one aimed to solve evacuation issues, to facilitate the location of shelters and to help finding transportation on the scene, if necessary. The post-hurricane scenarios respectively tackled in house donation pick-ups and debris clearing.


Iteration

Storyboards

After analyzing all the different scenario ideas and taking the personas into account, we narrowed down our ideas and created storyboards for those features we wanted in our app.

Speed Dating

With the storyboards in hand, the group conducted speed dating sessions with different users, to find out their reactions about each idea. With this method, we were able to check if our product would be of potential interest or not. We learned, for example, that potential users really cared about their safety when using the app and requested background clearance on volunteers.


Low-fidelity wireframe

We prototyped a low-fidelity version of our app, in order to rapidly vary interactions and to establish the app's flow.


Color-Scheme

While deciding the color scheme for our high-fidelity wireframes, we thought about a shade of red to symbolize emergency. However, in order to keep it color friendly, the team opted for a shade tending to pink. For background, in order to keep it clean and light, we opted for white. Remaining information was desingned to appear mostly in light and dark gray.
