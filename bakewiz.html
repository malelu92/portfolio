BakeWiz - Predicting Baking Machine Failures

Our goal was to provide Markel Baking Systems, a product-based company that sells baking machinery and systems, a service-based mobile-app that utilizes emerging technology. Markel's clients are composed by huge baking good companies, who can lose millions of dollars in case a single machine in the production system stops working. It's key then, that Markel offers a sevice that can predict machine failure, saving technician's long working hours and keeping customer satisfaction.

BakeWiz

After researching Markel's sale-cycle and customer base, as well as the value proposition of a mobile-app for Markel, we created BakeWiz, an app that utilizes predictive analytics to communicate machinery and system failure information to Markel's customers in an way that non-technicians and machine experts can understand. By collecting real machinery data from the clients during it's implementation, BakeWiz can use this analytics to point to technicians the most probable points of failure in the system when performance decreases. That way, a machine can be fixed before completely stopping and causing a block in production.




Research

General stakeholder model

We started with some background research on our client and their constituent companies, and then mapped out relevant stakeholders in Markel's business. Understanding the client gave us insight into problem spaces where a service-based app might give value to Markel and its customers.




Ideation

Assumptions and Reversed Assumptions

The team came up with 13 assumptions that we thought would be applicable to the client. We then reversed these assumptions, in order to visualize a scenario where the premises wouldn't be true and encouraged us to think about extreme solutions based on the reversed assumptions.




Questions

The team then focused on a problem obtained from a combination of assumptions: "Machines are Hard to Operate", meaning they are complex and present failure. This problem led us to 20 questions on possible modifications to solve it.




Collaborative Sketches of Concepts

The team discussed four possible solutions for the chosen problem:

  1. Educational Tool for Retailers
  2. Problem Failure Diagnostics
  3. Remote Control
  4. Factory Layout Design

For each of them, we collaboratively created a low-fidelity screen map with the flow and functionalities of each solution. Each team member had two minutes to collaborate to a map and then had to move to the next one.




Value Proposition

We evaluated the values offered for the client in our two most promissing ideas. We choose to do configuration system to increase the consulting efficiency between Markel and consumer.




Screen Flow

We discussed the screen flow, keeping in mind the main functions of our app and which screen would transition to another.




Iteration

Low-Fidelity Wireframe

We adapted the draft with the screen map to a low fidelity wireframe, where we could have a more clear view of how the flow would look.

Follow up Research

To validate our assumption about the purchasing process, we reached out to both the engineering team and sales team of Reading Bakery Systems, a branch of Markel Food Groups. Neither of them mentioned they observe any major breakdown of this system, and neither of them confirmed there would be great value added from our proposed mobile service. For this reason, we then decided to change our solution to our second most promising idea, diagnostics.


Revamping the Workflow

Our new workflow is based entirely on the idea of leading the user to the malfunctioning machine as quickly as possible. Instead of a holistic view of the system, we present only the potential breakdowns, then the user will be directed to the physical location of the machine and fixing instructions in the next screen.




Mobile Diagnostic Service

From our research, we learned that the production system Markel offers are large and complex. There are numerous break points that could impact production, yet there no good reporting system for technicians to locate these errors. Due to the short time-schedule and the change of direction, our first wireframes were already high-fidelity.





Computer Vision Microinteraction

We also wanted to demonstrate how the technician could use Computer Vision to spot external machinery problems.




High-Fidelity Screens

We then created our high-fidelity screens, which allow the user to easily navigate to the main possible cause of a system's decrease in production.



Road Map

Finally, in order to show future appliances and the evolution of our app, we created a roadmap.
