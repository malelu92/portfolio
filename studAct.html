Reinventing CMU's club search experience

University students highly value student clubs. Be it sports, music, politics or others, clubs are an important part of most students' college experience. Making sure that the right clubs can be easily found by students is interesting for both them and the clubs themselves, which can benefit from a higher number of students associates. In this project, I developed an app that improves the search and communication with clubs at Carnegie Mellon Univeristy.

ClubDiscover

ClubDiscover is an app that allows students to find the most suitable clubs based on their preferences and schedule and to easily obtain the desired information from club leaders. The app also keeps students updated all year long only with the events that fits their calendars and interests.

Research

Interview

With the goal of improving on-campus student activitiy experience, the team interviewed student club leaders and regular students. Our goal was to determine students' level of interest on clubs along with their previous experience on finding them. Moreover we wanted to learn more about the recruitment process, the ease to find new members and communication with current club members.


Sequence Flow and Cultural Models

Based on each interview, the team created sequence flows, to better understand the dinamics of club search, and cultural models simbolyzing the entities and relations involved. This helped us to better understand the problem context and to visualize it.




Affinity Diagram

Using the notes from each interview we divided them by similarities, using an affinity diagram. We first grouped them using blue labels that represented the essence and concept of these grouped notes. The blue groups were them grouped under pink labels, simbolyzing common issues, which were them grouped again into potential goals, simbolized by green labels.




After using the affinity diagram to understand the main issues for both students and clubs leaders, we consolidated the individual sequence flows and cultural models from each interviewee into a more general one. This permited again a more general understanding on the happening of events for club leader and students looking for clubs and how entities influence them and the process of joining clubs.




Ideation

Design Ideas

The team "walked the wall" by observing individually the affinity diagram, consolidated sequence flow and consolidated cultural model and thought about design ideas and breakdowns in each model. We grouped the ideas and breakdowns by similarities and created visions of the most popular ones. The visions were analyzed by adding a "+" on parts that we considered good, based on obtained data, "-" on attributes that were not technologically viable and "?" for questions brought by the visions.




Iteration

Storyboards

We improved each vision by solving the negative points and the questions previously stated. The team then created storyboards for each of these visions to help us better visualize our proposed solutions and to more easily communicate them to potential users.




Speed Dating

We conducted speed dating sessions with each storyboard. These speed dating sessions consisted of having the users read aloud the storyboards and to analyze their needs and reactions to the proposed solution. The information collected from users' reactions and comments helped us to polish the ideas.

Students' comments varied from finding the proposed solutions useful and others not that much. Students seemed excited about the idea of easily finding clubs that matched their schedules and interests and being able to keep tracking new events.


Paper prototype

Based on the feedback obtained, I designed a low-fidelity prototype to our most popular idea. This helped me understand if users could easily interact with the app and find the information they were looking for. One issue to be improved was making the event functionality more prominent in the app, since this is the main tool the team planned to use to have users interacting with our app all year long.





High-fidelity prototype

Sequentially to the low-fidelity prototype, I created a high-fidelity prototype incorporating the critique obtained from the previous one.
