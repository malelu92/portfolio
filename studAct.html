Simplifying the university club search

University students highly value student clubs. Be it sports, music, politics or others, clubs are an important part of most students' college experience. Making sure that the right clubs can be easily found by students is interesting for both them and the clubs themselves, which can benefit from a higher level of students asoociates. In this project, I developed an app that improves the search for clubs, benefiting both the students and the clubs.

Research

Interview

With the goal of improving on-campus student activitiy experience, me and my team conducted interviews on both student club leaders and regular students. With this, we wanted to find out from regular students how much students were interested on clubs, the time they search for clubs to join and how is or was their experience on joining activities. For club leaders we asked about the recruitment process of their clubs, the facility to find new members and communication with club members. Notes taken from the interviews were organized per each user, keeping one focus point on each different note.

Sequence Flow and Cultural Models

Based on each interview, the team created sequence flows, to better understand the dinamics of students looking for clubs and advertising them.

Current State Model

Based on the research findings, the team created a current state model, having our target group, earning teens, in the center and mapping which entities influence the group's spending habits. The influences are marked in the arrows, correlating each entity. The most interesting insight from this model is that we couldn't find a direct link between the teenagers and the bank, because most teenagers don't have bank accounts.
