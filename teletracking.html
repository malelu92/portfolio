TeleTracking - Helping victims of disasters

During times of man-made or natural disasters hopistals receive a surge of patients and non-patients looking for shelter and protection. Going to a hospital that is already at full capacity can cost the lives of injured patients. Informing citiznes the most appropriate location for them to take shelter is then crucial for lives to be saved and hospitals work at their best in order to save people. This project is currently being developed.

Understanding the Problem Space

Project Kickoff

We met with TeleTracking to better understand the problem space. We asked them questions about their main goals with the project, explained our design process and conducted a design activity with them: we asked them to create a TeleTracking persona, asking them to impersonate the company's main values and goals.




Initial Research Methods

The team decided to shape our first two months of work on doing guerilla research, secondary research and primary research.






Guerilla Research

By quickly talking to the average citizens who had already been through a disaster, some of our key findings were:



People might know what to do during a disaster, but when facing a disaster, they might panic and not follow the procedures.


Supermarkets can be used for shelter due to heat and electricity.


Disaster preparedness and knowledge often comes from school, movies and the news.

Ideation

Assumptions and Reversed Assumptions

The team came up with 13 assumptions that we thought would be applicable to the client. We then reversed these assumptions, in order to visualize a scenario where the premises wouldn't be true and encouraged us to think about extreme solutions based on the reversed assumptions.




Questions

The team then focused on a problem obtained from a combination of assumptions: "Machines are Hard to Operate", meaning they are complex and present failure. This problem led us to 20 questions on possible modifications to solve it.




Collaborative Sketches of Concepts

The team discussed four possible solutions for the chosen problem:

  1. Educational Tool for Retailers
  2. Problem Failure Diagnostics
  3. Remote Control
  4. Factory Layout Design

For each of them, we collaboratively created a low-fidelity screen map with the flow and functionalities of each solution. Each team member had two minutes to collaborate to a map and then had to move to the next one.




Value Proposition

We evaluated the values offered for the client in our two most promissing ideas. We choose to do configuration system to increase the consulting efficiency between Markel and consumer.




Screen Flow

We discussed the screen flow, keeping in mind the main functions of our app and which screen would transition to another.
