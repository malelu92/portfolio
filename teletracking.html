TeleTracking - Helping victims of disasters

During times of man-made or natural disasters hopistals receive a surge of patients and non-patients looking for shelter and protection. Going to a hospital that is already at full capacity can cost the lives of injured patients. Informing citiznes the most appropriate location for them to take shelter is then crucial for lives to be saved and hospitals work at their best in order to save people. This project is currently being developed.

Understanding the Problem Space

Project Kickoff

We met with TeleTracking to better understand the problem space. We asked them questions about their main goals with the project, explained our design process and conducted a design activity with them: we asked them to create a TeleTracking persona, asking them to impersonate the company's main values and goals.




Initial Research Methods

The team decided to shape our first two months of work on doing guerilla research, secondary research and primary research.






Guerilla Research

By quickly talking to the average citizens who had already been through a disaster, some of our key findings were:


When facing a disaster people might panic and not follow the procedures, even if they know them.

Supermarkets can be used for shelter due to heat and electricity.

Disaster preparedness and knowledge often comes from school, movies and the news.



Surveys

We publisehd a survey on MTurk hoping to obtain some more information on areas we still had a gap on data based on secondary research. The survey was answered by 98 people who had experienced a disaster.

We found out that hospitals were still a fairly popular shelter location.

We wanted to understand the main reasons that lead people to shelter in hospitals:




Interviews

We decided that for it was relevant to obtain more detailed information in specific areas. In order to obtain that information, we conducted interviews with:

  1. Disaster experts (Rubicon, Paragon, Red Cross...)
  2. Disaster survivors (from hurricane Sandy, hurricane Maria)
  3. Hospital workers
  4. Teletracking's clients

Interviewing staff from the hospitals who are TeleTracking's clients and specifically had asked for a disaster solution, we were able to gather many insights:

  1. Hospitals need a way to track in their facilities citizens who are not the hospital's patients.
  2. People with special needs are sent to the hospitals in case they special needs shelters are full. The hospitals will treat them, but they are not considered patients.
  3. Citizens many times won't take a disaster seriously. When they realize the need of shelter, the hospital might be the closest safe location.

Affinity Diagramming

In order to better understand all the information obtained in our interviews, we made an affinity diagram to cluster the information.




Design Sprints

Pitching ideas

In order to start generating solutions for our problem space, each person on the team had to create a solution and pitch it to the other, using the previous research as a base to justify why the proposed idea is good.




Stakeholder model and Crazy 8s activity

In order to generate more ideas in a faster and more creative way, we first used our stakeholder model to identify pain points between the entities and the values exchanged by them.



After that, we each generated 8 ideas in 8 minutes, which forced us to be creative and think out of the box. We then grouped these ideas into clusters.



Next Steps

We are currently in the process of meeting with TeleTracking to exposed our most promising ideas and get their feedback on it. For the next step we will narrow down the ideas even more and pick one to start developing low-fidelity wireframes.
