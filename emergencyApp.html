Hurricane Emergency App

Hurricanes hit the US every year. Whether they cause big destruction or only minor damages they scare citizens and bring uncertainty. LifeBoat is a peer-to-peer app that stimulates communication and help between the people of these cities.

LifeBoat

LifeBoat consists of an application that allows citizens of towns that can be or have been hit by hurricanes, to locate and/or drive other citizens to shelters and to help other members in the community with debris emergencies.


Research

Guerrilla Research

The team started with a broad scope problem of designing a peer economy app that would help victims of disasters. To better understand this scope we conducted guerrilla research trying to find out more information related to natural disasters in general and to understand how people who had been through them had to dealt with the situation.


Ideation

Scenarios

After finding out more about the needs and difficulties of people who pass through emergency situations and natural disasters the team created 20 scenarios representing possible solutions. These scenarios represented solutions tackling the previously identified issues and suggested schedules pick-ups for shelter transportation and donations, debris clearing and real-time donation monitoring.


Personas

In order to better understand future customers, our team created two personas, each representing one side of the peer economy app, the victim and provider. These personas helped us better visualize potential users and to develop final scenarios.

Jeff the volunteer

Linda the customer



Jeff, the volunteer, is 25 years old and lives in Immokalee, FL. He works in a local construction firm doing manual labor and drives a Ford F150 pickup truck. He has experienced three hurricanes during his time in Florida and has served as a volunteer after each one; he primarily transported people and heavy/large donations to the shelters and cleared large debris that blocked people from leaving their homes. Jeff helps people not only in his city, but also in the nearby ones.

Linda is 36 years old and lives in Naples, FL. She is a single mother of an 7 year old boy and a 8 year old girl and works as an administrative assistant in the local elementary school. Her mother is 75 and lives nearby in an apartment by herself. Because her mother has trouble walking, Linda and her kids visit her in her apartment regularly. She has been through many hurricanes and has never evacuated, she has visited a center for donation one time and usually brings her mom to her house during hurricanes.


Final Scenarios

With the personas in mind, the team adapted three final scenarios. Two related to post-hurricane situations and one related to a pre-hurricane situation.

The pre-hurricane one aimed to solve evacuation issues, facilitating the location of shelter and finding transportationt there in case needed. The post-hurricane ones tackled respectively in house donation pick-up in a scheduled time and debris clearing.


Iteration

Storyboards

After analyzing all the different scenario ideas and taking the personas into account, we narrowed down our ideas and created storyboards of the features we wanted to have in our app.

Speed Dating

With the storyboards in hand the group conducted speed dating sessions with different users, to find out their reactions about each idea. With this method, we were able to see if the product we created could potentially interest users. We learned for example that potential users really cared about their safety on using that app and requested a service that provided background checks on volunteers.


Low-fidelity wireframe

We prototyped a low-fidelity version of our app, in order to rapidly vary interactions and the app's flow.


Color-Scheme

When deciding the color scheme for our high-fidelity wireframes, we wanted to choose a shade of red, which, we believe, symbolizes emergency. In order to keep it a more friendly color, the team opted for a shade tending to pink. In order to have a clean and light background, we chose to have a white background, with other informations given mainly in black and gray.
